# HR Analytics Dashboard (Power BI)

## 📊 Project Overview

This project analyzes **employee attrition and workforce demographics** using **Power BI**.
The dashboard helps identify patterns in employee turnover based on **age, salary, education, job role, and department**.

The goal of this analysis is to help organizations understand **why employees leave and which groups are most affected**, enabling better HR decision-making.

---

## 🛠 Tools & Technologies Used

* **Power BI**
* **Power Query (Data Cleaning)**
* **DAX Measures**
* **Data Visualization**

---

## 📁 Dataset

The dataset contains HR information about employees including:

* Employee demographics
* Job roles and departments
* Salary ranges
* Education levels
* Attrition status
* Age groups

This data is used to analyze **attrition trends and workforce characteristics**.

## 🔍 Key Insights

### 👥 Workforce Overview

* Total Employees: **961**
* Employees who left the company: **133**
* Overall Attrition Rate: **13.8%**
* Average Employee Age: **37 years**
* Average Salary: **6.28K**
* Average Years at Company: **6.86 years**

---

### 🎓 Attrition by Education

Employees with **Education Level 3 (42%)** show the highest attrition.
Education Levels **4 (24%)** and **2 (17%)** also contribute significantly to employee turnover.

This indicates that employees with mid-level qualifications may have **more external job opportunities**.

---

### 👨‍💼 Attrition by Age Group

The **26–35 age group** experiences the highest attrition with **67 employees leaving**.

Other age groups:

* 36–45 → 25 employees
* 18–25 → 24 employees
* 46–55 → 9 employees
* 55+ → 8 employees

This suggests that **early to mid-career professionals are the most likely to change jobs**.

---

### 💰 Attrition by Salary

Most attrition occurs among employees earning **Up to 5K salary (110 employees)**.

Other salary ranges show significantly lower attrition:

* 5K–10K → 12 employees
* 10K–15K → 8 employees
* 15K+ → 3 employees

This indicates that **lower salary levels are strongly linked with higher attrition**.

---

### 🧑‍🔬 Attrition by Job Role

Job roles with the highest attrition include:

* **Laboratory Technician → 62**
* **Research Scientist → 47**
* **Manufacturing Director → 10**
* **Healthcare Representative → 9**

These roles may experience **higher workload or competitive job markets**.

---

### 👨‍💻 Attrition by Gender

Attrition among **male employees (88)** is significantly higher compared to **female employees (38)**.

---

## 🚀 Conclusion

The analysis reveals that **employee attrition is highest among early-career professionals, lower salary employees, and certain technical job roles**.

Organizations can reduce attrition by:

* Improving compensation for lower salary groups
* Enhancing career development opportunities for employees aged **26–35**
* Focusing retention strategies on high-turnover roles such as **Laboratory Technicians and Research Scientists**
